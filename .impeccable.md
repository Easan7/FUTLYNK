## Design Context

### Users
Young working adults and students (18-35) who play casual to semi-competitive futsal regularly and need reliable game coordination. Their core job is to quickly find, join, and manage games with the right skill level and dependable players while minimizing coordination overhead. The product must support discovery of nearby games, trust signals (quality/reliability), group availability coordination, and personal progression tracking.

### Brand Personality
Competitive, sharp, grounded (with an urban edge). The interface should feel like a real sports environment: direct, fast, and credible, without social fluff or futuristic gimmicks.

### Aesthetic Direction
Mobile-first, minimal, structured, high-contrast UI with strong sports identity. Layouts should be compact and information-dense with clear primary/secondary hierarchy. Use restrained color: predominantly dark neutrals with intentional lime accent usage. Subtle identity cues such as pitch-line textures and scoreboard-like stats are welcome when functional. Avoid gradients (especially blue/purple SaaS palettes), glassmorphism, glow-heavy neon effects, bubbly rounding, crypto/AI dashboard aesthetics, decorative-only elements, and oversized marketing hero patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Optimize for coordination speed: primary actions must be obvious and reachable in seconds.
2. Build trust through clarity: reliability, fit, and status should be explicit and easy to verify.
3. Keep visual discipline: one accent, minimal noise, strong hierarchy, compact spacing.
4. Design for real mobile usage: thumb-friendly targets, interruption-tolerant flows, readable dense information.
5. Ship practical accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA contrast, reduced-motion support, color-independent meaning, and robust touch target sizing.
